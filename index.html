About Me

A young innovator pushing boundaries in programming language design and computing education

Born in 2010 in Kerala, India, I'm a self-taught programmer and technologist who began developing software and studying advanced computer science concepts before the age of 15. My work focuses on making computing more accessible through thoughtful design and cognitive-load optimization.

I'm the creator of Jam, a beginner-friendly programming language designed with learning psychology in mind. I've authored its formal specification including grammar, operational semantics, interpreter, and compiler. My work has received direct feedback and encouragement from leading computer scientists like Matthias Felleisen and Vint Cerf.

Beyond language design, I've built modern web applications, developer tools, and AI-powered systems. I'm particularly interested in the intersection of programming languages, human-computer interaction, and education technology.
